Project 0: Becoming Friends with Your Camera

CS 280A — Computer Vision and Computational Photography

Overview

This project explores how distance, focal length, and camera motion affect perception. Through three mini-experiments we compare portraits at different distances/zooms, observe perspective compression in architecture, and create a dolly-zoom sequence.

Part 1 — Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Goal: demonstrate why stepping back and zooming in yields more natural facial proportions than a close wide-angle shot.

Notes
  • Keep the face roughly the same size by moving back and increasing focal length.
  • Compare features (nose/ears) to illustrate perspective distortion from camera distance.

Part 2 — Architectural Perspective Compression

Goal: show how telephoto vs. wide-angle alters perceived depth. Telephoto “compresses” distances; wide-angle emphasizes depth.

Part 3 — The Dolly Zoom Effect

Move the camera back while zooming in so the subject stays the same size but the background stretches. Stitch the frames into an animation.

Capture tips
  • Mark the subject to keep framing consistent between shots.
  • Increase focal length as you step back; keep exposure consistent if possible.
  • Assemble frames into a GIF/MP4; optionally stabilize in post.

Methods

  • Distance vs. focal length: maintain subject size while changing camera distance to separate perspective from magnification.
  • Perspective compression: capture a scene twice — far+telephoto and near+wide — matching framing as closely as possible.
  • Dolly zoom: collect a sequence with incremental position/zoom changes; compose as GIF (or MP4 for smaller files).
