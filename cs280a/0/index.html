CS 280A — Computer Vision and Computational Photography

Part 1 — Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Notes
  • When you take a selfie up close, the camera is so close to your face that it distorts your features — your nose can look bigger, your forehead might seem stretched, and your face shape can look a little off.
  • But when you step back a few feet and zoom in to make your face the same size in the frame, the photo looks way better. That’s because zooming in flattens the perspective, making your features look more balanced and natural
